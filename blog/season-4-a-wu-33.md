10 - Luke (NEW) - League A - Last 5: 37 (D), 56 (W), 44 (W), 36 (L), 58 (W) - Welcome Luke to the list! Finally he has escaped 'On the bubble (in no order)' purgatory and found his way here after a 58 point week and a pretty solid run of form. This week's performance was brought to you by Mats Wieffer though, who played right back (as a MID) and found himself scoring a brace and also hitting defcon. And he's got a double gameweek this week. Is he the best player in the Premier League? I'm sure Luke thinks so. Anyway, Thiago has also been finding form once again, Groß was perhaps the signing of the Winter, and Senesi is back to his defensive contributing ways. Plus only one of Isak and Ekitike is allowed to be healthy at a time and the dice has rolled Luke's way (for now).

9 - Alex (+1) - League B - Last 5: 39 (L), 43 (W), 60 (W), 28 (W), 61 (W) - A second 60+ week in the last 3 for Alex, this time he earned 33 points from 3 players as he welcomed Roefs back into the lineup. His 11 points were joined by fellow Sunderland clean sheeter Alderete (8 points) and Bowen, who of course had 14. Still a few solid scores around, including Konate and Kroupi each picking up 6 and Truffert and Szoboszlai in the 4-5 range. Maybe having 7 players from 2 teams does work out?

8 - Kevin (-) - League A - Last 5: 37 (D), 51 (W), 45 (W), 24 (L), 51 (W) - The first of a surprising number of 51 pointers this week, Kevin had a plethora of solid performances, including 9 points from Bart and KDH, 8 from Doku, and 5-6 pointers from Mitchell, Frimpong, and Gyokeres. 7 - Zach (-) - League A - Last 5: 54 (W), 31 (L), 33 (L), 33 (W), 51 (W) - A huge bounce back to save his spot on this list and take his place at the top of League A after some doubt may have been cast on his team over the past few weeks. Jokes aside, an assist from Morgan Rogers in back to back matches, a couple extra clean sheets from Ferdi and Nunes, and another couple defcon returns from Sangare and Gomez made up for the fact that Saka is in confusing Arteta press conference land and Ekitike is now out for the season.

6 - Jesse (-) - League A - Last 5: 39 (W), 55 (W), 39 (W), 49 (W), 43 (L) - Despite the loss and his winstreak coming to an end, Jesse managed to overcome the adversity and leave 17 points on his bench and still put up 43 points. A solid week to have double Liverpool defense + Casemiro. A rough week to own a Wolves defender and Romero (who was badly injured vs Sunderland). Anyway, this is Glasner's favorite time of the year so JSL and Sarr will probably be the best 2 players to own in the game.

5 - Darryan (-3) - League A - Last 5: 43 (W), 65 (W), 34 (L), 28 (L), 32 (L) - 3 losses in 3 and not a score over 34 since his 65 pointer definitely puts some question marks around Darryan's title chances in the run in. That said, if he can figure out his bench, Xhaka, Scott, Rodri, and Haaland ahead of a couple Arsenal defenders and Kelleher seems like it could be enough to take him to the promised land (if he can beat Zach in their head to head).

4 - Dylan (+1) - League A - Last 5: 48 (L), 40 (L), 62 (W), 38 (W), 47 (W) - Another solid high 40 point week from Dylan as he went unpunished for benching Mukiele's 14 pointer due to the Janelt injury. Still though, he keeps putting up scores and keeps winning so he's within striking distance of Zach, even.

3 - John (+1) - League B - Last 5: 41 (W), 59 (W), 36 (W), 32 (L), 51 (W) - The other Gabriel owner _also_ enjoyed a 14 pointer from a different defender (Nico O'Reilly). So honestly, maybe John should switch with Dylan for not being able to sign into his account last week to change his lineup?
