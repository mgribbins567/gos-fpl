Okay so this one might resonate more with you format-heads, because I went a _little_ wild with it. Two group stages. No eliminations until the playoffs. A single elimination round of 16 which led to double elimination matchups for the remainder of the tournament.

Basically, the first group stage placed managers into their second group stage groups. I.e. all 1st placed finishers went to Group E, all 2nd placed finishers went to Group F, and so on. This allowed me to seed based the Round of 16 in two ways:

1. By 2nd Group
2. By Finish

So, every 1st Group Stage top place finisher would play a bottom place finisher, based on how they did in the 2nd Group Stage. The idea was that the 2nd Group Stage would have these really interesting, evenly weighted matches because they'd be between managers in similar form. And I guess it didn't really? work because 3/4 of the 2nd group stage groups had a winner on 9 points while the 1st group stage only had one manager on 9 points.

Anyway, Luke won this one by defeating Kevin 91-81 in the final.

### Season 4: The League

So, this tournament was one of the things I was the most excited about when expanding to two leagues (/24 managers). And honestly, I think it went pretty well! A 24 team group stage in which each manager played 8 matches seeded 2 rounds of playoffs in which the winners of those played against the top 4 teams.

I wanted to use this section to sidetrack a little bit (as I come back to writing this ~1 day later), and sort of deep dive this. Because I _really_ like the idea of a big group stage table like this. I was skeptical at first when UEFA made this change, but after both seeing it in action and playing with a team in it a bit in Football Manager, I think it was honestly a really great change. I think the balance between drama at the end of the group stage (will I make the cut) and the forgiveness that having 8 whole gameweeks to do well and see your team find form and battle it out against a breadth of opponent makes for some really _interesting_ matches. And also I find group stages kinda fun in FPL (and apparently love coming up with ways to schedule matches) so I figured maybe this could be a good idea! Definitely let me know your feedback and if you enjoyed it or thought it was too long!

Anyway, one metric(if that's the right word) that I think we can look at to gauge just how _dramatic_ a group stage was is table movement. So I've compiled a little table to show this.

- The biggest movement after gameweek 4 (exclusive) was: Alex +6 in GW5, Darryan -6 in GW5, Matthew +5 in GW5, MatthewR -5 in GW7, ZachA -4 in GW5, Zach +4 in GW7, ZachA and Mack +4 in GW8
- MatthewR went from 2nd with 2 matches left to 9th in the final league table

I have to move on because I have a lot more recap to get to, but I feel like some takeaways from this are:

- The group stage could have been shorter. As much as I'm glad my team made top 4 in the final week, we also had 2 pretty boring weeks after GW6.
- The top teams were _really_ close after both 6 and 8 weeks. 5 managers were at 15 points after GW6 and 18 after GW8. In fact, the top 4 was determined by PF in both weeks.
- There comes a time when the worse teams just have no chance. GW5 was the last time the bottom 8 changed.

So, next season. I want to brainstorm some ideas to increase/keep the amount of competition and closeness at the top, while also balancing having more of a battle for the lower playoff spots at the top 16 cutoff point.

The knockout rounds came with three unique twists: a "break" from GW9-GW20, 2 playoff rounds, and our first knockout stage tiebreaker. I think the break was a cool way to mimic the real life Champions League format. It gave teams the chance to evolve and find form to bounce back for the playoffs. However, I do think I could have done a better job at making it clear what cup was going on, considering the Boxing Day Bash was played during The League break.

I also really enjoyed how the 2 playoff rounds / bye weeks situation went. It felt like a nice way to mirror the real life CL (again), plus gave me a sort of qualifying round vibe. Plus, it obviously worked as a story teller, considering Andrew made a run from Playoff 1 to make the semi finals, and Matthew2 made the finals from Playoff 1.

And that brings me to the final twist: Andrew. Somehow in Playoff 1, he absolutely crushed fellow League B contender ZachA 44-25. He then followed it up with a 41-39 win against Luke in Playoff 2. And then, he met League A champion Zach in the quarter final and _tied_ 56-56 over two legs. Our first ever instance of a two-legged draw in Game of Stones history. So, I decided that they'd take penalties by choosing 5 players to face off in a tiebreaker, which Andrew won 37-21.

I think this was a clever and interesting way to break the tie (thanks Darryan!), but the fact it took up an extra gameweek made the season feel just a tad bit more crammed, I must admit.

Anyway, Dylan spoiled Andrew's fairytale run and then beat Matthew2 in the final 80-74.

The next couple of tables to look at are our remaining Copium tables, the xP and advanced xP (or, all managers) tables. As a quick reminder, the xP table is manager vs average every week, while the All Managers table is managers vs every other manager every week. So xP is a measure of consistently beating the average, while the AM table is an actual count of how many managers you _would have_ beaten every week.

Anyway, Zach unsurprisingly topped both of these tables with a pretty sizeable 6 point gap (also, how much he won the league by). After that though, these tables have a bit of a disagreement on who was better. Here's a list so we can visualize it (amP is all managers points):

Alright, so the tables are great at determining the 1st and last placed teams. Other than that, due to the fine margins of FPL and the fact that 1 point or 1 matchup can change the table so drastically, it's hard to use these as a predictor past general place. For example, Darryan's actual finish was 3rd and he finished 4th on xP and 2nd on AM, Kevin finished 4th when he was 2nd on xP and 4th on AM, and Luke finished 5th with a 3rd place xP finish and 7th on AM. So the ranges are for the most part correct, but you still end up with Jesse's 2nd place finish while 8th on xP and 6th on AM. Or even in a lesser sense, Dylan's 6th place finish at 5th on xP and 3rd on AM. And Dylan finished in the _top half_ of the luck table.

So all that to say, FPL is a crapshoot sometimes. You have to play the cards you're dealt and sometimes you're gonna lose a lot or win a lot despite your actions week to week.
