Hello everyone and welcome to our final weekly update! I hope you had a blast this season and will return ([fill out this form pls](https://forms.gle/Bih56H77FT7Gwoav7)) and/or bring along a friend ([make them fill out this form pls](https://forms.gle/rgXQcnJFHAoTb3AL9)). This weekly update will be pretty normal, but I'll spend a little more time putting together a proper season recap (for both leagues!) and send that out probably later this week!

Anyway! The final gameweek came with some very fun results as a lot of teams didn't have anything to play for (or did, in a few days) and rested/rotated. The closest match this week was, of course, Jesse vs Gavin, in which the former won 24-22. Luke also spoiled Zach's victory lap with his 46-43 win, I stole away Darryan's 2nd place finish by defeating him 36-31, and Scott left a League A parting gift with a 60 point week to defeat MatthewR's 44. Full results are as follows:

- Darryan 31-36 Matthew

Game Changers! The 10-screen set up proved itself worthy this week so I should have seen some action from every player here.

- Matthew: Taty - 8 pts - A huge header on a corner to start the scoring for West Ham against Leeds was all for nought as Everton failed to beat Spurs smh.
- Jesse: Adrien Truffert - 6 pts - Truffert's 6th assist of the season came from a pretty solid finish from Tavernier as Bournemouth failed to defeat Nottingham Forest but didn't need to since Brentford also failed to defeat Liverpool.
- Dylan: Mohamed Salah - 6 pts - One final appearance on this list from the FPL king himself following his assist to Curtis Jones this weekend earning him, of course, 1 bonus point as well.
- Luke: Pedro Porro - 10 pts - A huge week for Porro as somehow the attacking fullback turned defender (again) and hit defcon and picked up 2 bonus points as Everton sadly failed to score once against Spurs, let alone twice.
- Matt: Jarrod Bowen - 12 pts - Almost half of Matt's points came from Bowen as he picked up his 9th goal and 13th assist of the season against Leeds this week to make him the only player to be in both the 24/25 and 25/26 FPL team of the season.
- Scott: Patrick Dorgu - 18 pts - A huge performance from Dorgu this week as he scored Bruno's 21st (official) assist, assisted another goal that I can't remember off the top of my head, and kept a clean sheet against famously toothless Brighton.

Congratulations to Zach for winning his _5th_ Manager of the Month this season! He took the 1st place spot on PF ahead of Dylan, me, Luke, and Jesse!

10 - Matthew (NEW) - League A - Last 5: 27 (W), 42 (L), 60 (W), 40 (W), 36 (W) - Definitely no bias here (I did compare my scores vs like 4 other managers before deciding tho) but ending the season with 4/5 wins with the only loss coming from a 42 point week is pretty solid. Semenyo ended the season with a goal, as did all-star 12th/13th pick (I can't remember) Mateta, and West Ham 2nd best signing of the season Taty. Plus, Enzo Le Fee picked up 3 bonus points with 0 goals/assists/defcon points in a 2-1 match in which Sunderland defeated Chelsea. So he's probably the best midfielder to play in Europe next season if I had to guess.

9 - Kevin (-2) - League A - Last 5: 34 (W), 60 (W), 54 (W), 27 (L), 22 (L) - A rough end to the season as Kevin limps home to a very respectable 4th place finish. This week's highlight came from his own namesake, Kevin from Fulham, who hit the crossbar on a direct free kick and was awarded an FPL assist when Diop headed home the rebound. He was then subbed off at 59' to deny himself and our Kevin the 2 more points he would have got if he had stayed on the pitch longer. This week's lowlight came from Morgan Gibbs-White, whose 9 points sat despairingly 3rd on Kevin's bench. Banking them for next year I guess.

8 - Luke (+2) - League A - Last 5: 48 (L), 45 (W), 40 (W), 25 (L), 46 (W) - A solid win for Luke this week to bounce back from, well, whatever happened in GW37, as he did what Crystal Palace couldn't and beat the crowned champion of the season. And he did so with a very solid score, off the back of Pedro Porro's 8th defcon of the season (wouldn't have expected him to reach the threshold that many times before this season started) and yet another Spurs clean sheet (thanks Kinsky :(). Palmer also finally proved he can do just as well as Mavropanos, as they both scored 8 this week. Joke's on Cole though, he was still outscored across the entire season by the West Ham centerback, 122-114. Oh and there was also the Lammens clean sheet points since apparently Gross decided he's done enough for the team this season.

7 - Mack (-3) - League B - Last 5: 62 (W), 46 (W), 46 (L), 17 (L), 26 (L) - A similar end of the season for the other Gyokeres owner as yet again, not much going for Mack's team other than the Semenyo goal and Milenkovic+Garner defcon returns. Doesn't help that Andersen decided a red card could be fun last week (Fulham kept a clean sheet) and Carrick decided Mazraoui could be fun instead of Dalot (United kept a clean sheet). But also Woltemade and Strand Larsen both started in a week for maybe the 2nd time this season?

6 - Chris (+3) - League B - Last 5: 32 (W), 37 (L), 62 (W), 49 (W), 47 (W) - A very solid finish to the season as Chris put up the highest League B score off the back of Ollie Watkins' 15th and 16th league goals as Aston Villa defeat Manchester City in Pep's final game. That's 5 goals (and an assist) in 3 games to end the season for the striker who was left out of the England squad in favor of Ivan Toney smh. Anyway, Watkins was joined by Mateus Fernandes's solid 9 point performance, Diouf's clean sheet, and the Wilson of the Callum variety's goal as well. Really just a day for purple kitted players on Chris's team.

5 - Darryan (+1) - League A - Last 5: 24 (L), 36 (W), 47 (W), 50 (L), 31 (L) - Two losses to end the season as Haaland gets benched for the 2nd time in 3 weeks (he's scored in every game in the last 7 he's started) and Darryan is forced to rely on his own purple players for points. 6 points from Summerville's assist and 7 from Hermansen's clean sheet join Shaw's (red is kinda purple) clean sheet as the only returns Darryan's got. And man, he really could've used the (at least) 6 points from a Haaland goal.

4 - Matthew2 (-1) - League B - Last 5: 40 (W), 40 (W), 40 (W), 51 (L), 31 (W) - Some may say my waivers may have been better if I wasn't on vacation, but I did pick up a win to give myself the faintest shot at winning League B. But alas, not enough, as despite Salah getting one final assist before sailing off into the sunset and Harry Maguire keeping one final clean sheet before getting as many minutes in the World Cup as me, John beat Cara which meant it didn't even really matter. Also special thanks to James Hill, who got recalculated to hit the defcon threshold and get me above 30 points this week.

3 - Alex (+2) - League B - Last 5: 31 (L), 58 (W), 51 (L), 43 (W), 42 (W) - A solid finale for Alex as he confirmed his spot in League A next season with the win this week. His also heavily purpled players did the heavy lifting, led by Bowen's 12 pointer. And you may disagree but if Manchester United red is close enough to purple then _surely_ red with black stripes is at least a little bit closer to purple, which is why I think Tavernier's 10 pointer and Truffert's 6 can be considered points for the purple shirts. And there is no comment section so you can't even disagree HAH. Anyway nobody else on his team did anything worth more than a 3 pointer (and that was only Collins).

2 - John (-) - League B - Last 5: 31 (L), 36 (L), 59 (W), 58 (W), 43 (W) - John's League B winning season comes to an end with a 3 winstreak where he averaged just over 50 points each of those weeks. This one came from (purple shirt) Bruno Fernandes's record breaking 21st (official) assist (24th FPL assist) and 9th goal of the season, totalling 14 points this week. He also saw the very purple double West Ham clean sheet and, probably the least purple of them all, Enzo Le Fee's bizarre 5 point performance against Chelsea. We can't even talk about Mateta's goal as he sits 3rd on John's bench. It would've been so good too because red + blue = purple.

1 - Zach (-) - League A - Last 5: 37 (W), 43 (W), 61 (W), 72 (W), 43 (L) - Yeah he lost but that's kinda the nature of the Zach vs Luke matchup: it's either a blowout or 3 point game. Anyway, despite the loss this week the League A winner kept his scores at 43+ in all of the last 4 weeks and that's despite having 2 0s on the field this week. He has purple players who returned who were already mentioned - Tavernier, Mateus Fernandes, Maguire - plus definitely (unfortunately) _not purple_ Danso who picked up 8 points. Maybe Harry Maguire _wasn't_ the key.
